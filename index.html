          How to read UC results: Access UC vs Floor-Guarded UC

          All UC campuses belong to the same system‚Äîbut they do not admit students the same way. This tool uses two terms to explain the difference so families can plan realistically.

          Access UC (Example: UC Merced)

          An Access UC is a campus where your student‚Äôs GPA band is commonly represented among admitted students. Admission does not depend heavily on major choice at the first-year level. Strategy is clean execution: meet UC requirements, submit a strong application, and avoid unnecessary academic risk.

          Floor-Guarded UC (Examples: UCR, UCSC, UCSD, UCLA)

          A Floor-Guarded UC applies strong GPA thresholds (even if they aren‚Äôt published). Applicants below that floor are admitted in small numbers regardless of activities or essays. Treat these as constrained or long-shot options if GPA is below the historical range, and prioritize reliable pathways alongside them.

          These labels describe campus behavior, not student ability. They do not guarantee outcomes.
